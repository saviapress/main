Modal verbs

Escoge la mejor opción de acuerdo al contexto.

 
  1. I didn’t feel very well yesterday. I ______________ eat anything.
    1.   cannot
    2.   couldn’t
    3.   mustn’t
  2. You _________ look at me when I am talking to you.
    1.   could
    2.   should
    3.   would
  3. I was using my pencil a minute ago. It ___________ be here somewhere!
    1.   can
    2.   could
    3.   must
    4.   would
  4. You really __________ be late again.
    1.   must not
    2.   don’t have to be
  5. If you don’t start working harder, you will ___________ repeat the course next year.
    1.   have to
    2.   must
    3.   will have to
  6. His parents spoil him. He’s always ____________ to do whatever he wants.
    1.   been able
    2.   been allowed
  7. Phone her now. She _____________ home by now.
    1.   has to be
    2.   must be
    3.   would be
  8. You ____________ forget your sun cream. It’s going to be very hot!
    1.   don’t have to
    2.   mustn’t
    3.   needn’t
  9. I ____________ be able to help you, but I’m not sure yet.
    1.   might
    2.   would
  10. Entrance to the museum was free. We _______________ pay to get in.
    1.   needn’t
    2.   didn’t need to
  11. Already as a child Mozart __________ play the piano beautifully.
    1.   could
    2.   should
    3.   would
  12. Which sign are you more likely to see at an airport: Bags __________ not be left unattended.
    1.   can
    2.   must
    3.   may
  13. I really _______________ try to get fit.
    1.   may
    2.   must
    3.   would
  14. ______________ take a photograph of you?
    1.   Am I allowed to
    2.   May I
  15. Students _____________ borrow up to 6 books at any time.
    1.   are allowed to
    2.   could

6.     Must     ó     Have to

Must y Have to traducen "Debes" ó "Tienes". Son verbos que indican obligación:

Must:       Es para obligaciónes que uno mismo se impone, normas/leyes, recomendaciones y probabilidades casi seguras.

Have to:   Es para obligaciones que vienen de afuera

A veces sirve usar ambas sin cambio en significado.

Esto aplica a la mayoria de los casos (~90%). Hay excepciones. Lo mejor es aprénderselas dentro de una frase, en contexto
